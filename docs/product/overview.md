# Product Overview

Ágora is an **open source (MIT), self-hostable knowledge platform** that
turns an organization's scattered information — manuals, meeting minutes,
business rules, requirements, decisions, and technical documentation —
into **queryable operational context** through natural language.

Its real product is not "chatting with documents": it is **managing
organizational context** in a traceable way, keeping full control of data,
infrastructure, and AI provider in the hands of the user.

## The problem

In most organizations, the knowledge needed to work correctly is
fragmented: partly in documents, partly in meetings, partly in messages,
and largely in the memory of a few key people. This causes:

- **Dependency on key employees** — a handful of people hold the context
  everyone else needs.
- **Knowledge loss** — when someone leaves, what they knew leaves with them.
- **Slow onboarding** — new members spend weeks reconstructing context that
  already exists, scattered.
- **Decisions made without context** — because finding the relevant
  background costs more than deciding blind.
- **Contradictory requirements** — new requirements clash with existing
  business rules nobody remembered.
- **Time wasted searching** — information exists but is not findable when
  it matters.

## The value proposition

> Turn an organization's scattered knowledge into accessible, verifiable,
> and useful context for its members.

Ágora lets an authorized person understand a project without fully
depending on the people who originally built it. This improves:

- knowledge transfer,
- operational continuity,
- decision quality,
- requirement design,
- onboarding,
- and cross-team collaboration.

## How it works (in one paragraph)

Members upload knowledge sources (PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, CSV, JSON) into
**projects** owned by their **organization**. Ágora processes each source
into semantically searchable fragments. When someone asks a question, the
platform retrieves the most relevant fragments, sends them to the
configured AI provider as grounding context, and returns an answer **with
citations to the exact sources used**. Conversations are persisted per
user and per project, and every part of the UI updates in real time.

## What Ágora is *not*

- **Not just a chat-with-documents** — the unit of value is managed,
  traceable organizational context, not a chat box.
- **Not a file search engine** — it answers questions; it does not rank
  files.
- **Not a Google Drive replacement** — it does not aim to be where files
  live; it is where knowledge becomes queryable.
- **Not a notes app** — sources are curated organizational knowledge, not
  personal scratchpads.
- **Not a traditional document manager** — no folder trees, versioning
  workflows, or approval chains.
- **Not its own AI model** — Ágora is the knowledge platform; the AI
  provider is interchangeable.
- **Not a replacement for human judgment** — it grounds decisions in
  context; it does not make them.
