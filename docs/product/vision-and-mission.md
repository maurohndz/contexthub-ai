# Vision & Mission

## The core idea

> An organization's knowledge should not disappear when a person leaves,
> stay hidden inside documents, or depend on who remembers a meeting.

Everything in Ágora follows from that sentence. Knowledge that lives only
in people's heads or in unread documents is a liability; knowledge that is
loaded, connected, and queryable is an asset that survives turnover,
reorganizations, and time.

## Mission

Turn an organization's scattered knowledge into **accessible, verifiable,
and useful context** for its members — while the organization keeps full
control of its data, its infrastructure, and its AI provider.

## Vision

A workplace where:

- understanding a project does not require finding the person who built it,
- decisions are made with the relevant context at hand — with sources,
- new members become productive by asking, not by archaeology,
- and nothing important is lost when someone walks out the door.

## Why open source and self-hostable

Organizational knowledge is among the most sensitive data an organization
owns. Ágora's promise only holds if:

- **the platform can be audited** (open source, MIT),
- **the data never has to leave the organization** (self-hostable,
  including fully local AI via Ollama),
- **no single AI vendor owns the relationship** (the provider is
  interchangeable by design).

Privacy is a structural capability of the product, not a pricing tier.
